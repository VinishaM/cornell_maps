War and Unrest

Word War I meant major changes to student life at Cornell. University students were extremely active in the war effort - Mary Merritt Crawford, Class of 1904, served as "house surgeon" in France, and was commissioned a first lieutenant in French Army for her work. Anna Irene von Sholly, 1902, was awarded the Croix de Guerre by the French government. Edward Tinkham, 1916, also received a Croix de Guerre, and went on to recruit a unit of fellow Cornellians that became the first Americans fighting in the war.

Back on campus, a U.S. Army School of Military Aeronautics was formed, and every male on campus was required to take 2 years of military training courses. Cornell commissioned more officers in World War I than any other American institution, including military academies. The political and social unrest following the war was present in the campus community as veterans returned to Cornell.
